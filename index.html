Seven Feet Travel Guide

Sydney

Harbour drama, coastal walks, easy ferries, excellent dining, and neighborhoods that reward a slower look.

Email guide

Why Go

Sydney mixes harbour icons with ferries, coastal walks, and neighborhoods worth wandering. The CBD and Circular Quay anchor the Opera House, Harbour Bridge, museums, and ferry connections, while beaches like Bondi and Manly balance city energy with ocean air. For official inspiration and trip ideas, browse Sydney on Visit NSW.

Late summer (February and March) runs hot and humid with intense UV; May through June cools with some rain; July and August stay mild by US standards, with nights rarely far below 10°C (50°F).

Getting There & Around

Nonstop flights from the US. Sydney Kingsford Smith (SYD) has nonstop options including Los Angeles (Qantas, United, American codeshare, Delta; about 15 to 16 hours), San Francisco (Qantas, United; about 16 to 17 hours), Dallas-Fort Worth (Qantas A380; about 17 hours), Houston (United seasonal; about 17.5 hours), and Honolulu (Qantas; Alaska for connections). LAX usually offers the most competition on fare; DFW on Qantas works well for a single-carrier trip with mileage earning.

Airport to city. SYD sits about 8 km south of the CBD. The Airport Link train reaches Central in about 13 minutes (runs roughly every 10 minutes; expect about AUD $19 to $21 including the airport station surcharge). Taxis or rideshares take 30 to 60 minutes depending on traffic (budget roughly AUD $45 to $65). Book a private transfer if you want a guaranteed pickup after a long haul or an early arrival.

Opal and transit. Pick up an Opal card at the airport, convenience stores, or newsagents; tap on and off every ride on trains, buses, light rail, and ferries. Daily caps are near AUD $18.70, weekly near AUD $50, with lower weekend caps on Saturdays and Sundays. Trains on the T-series cover the CBD and inner suburbs (T4 helps for Kings Cross and Bondi Junction). Light rail L2/L3 runs along George Street toward Circular Quay. Ferries are ideal for Manly, Taronga Zoo, and Balmain. Bus 311 connects Oxford Street to Kings Cross in about 10 minutes. Uber and Didi operate citywide. Driving downtown is rarely worth it: parking is pricey and traffic bites, so save a rental car for regional day trips.

Health & Safety

  • Sun and water Australian UV is strong even when it looks cloudy; wear SPF 30+, a hat, and sunglasses daily. Tap water is safe to drink across Sydney.SunSmart
  • Emergencies Dial 000 for police, fire, or ambulance. For non-urgent police matters call 131 444.
  • Medical costs Public hospitals run emergency departments, but bills can add up fast for visitors, so keep travel medical coverage active.healthdirect Australia
  • Staying alert Sydney is generally safe; still watch bags at Circular Quay and Bondi, stay aware late night around Kings Cross, and stick to well-lit routes along Oxford Street after dark.

Best For

  • Harbour-first itineraries plus beach time
  • Ferry-heavy exploring (Manly, Taronga, Balmain)
  • LGBTQ+ travelers and Sydney Gay and Lesbian Mardi Gras season (late February / early March)
  • Vivid Sydney visitors (May through June) and summer sculpture crowds along the coast

When to Go

Sydney Festival lights up January with arts programming citywide. New Year's Eve demands early harbour bookings. Sculpture by the Sea spans Bondi to Tamarama in October and November. The Biennale of Sydney runs every two years. Layer February through March for heat and UV; pack layers May through August.

Paul's Planning Note

Do not pack every day. Sydney is at its best when you leave space for ferries, coastal walks, long lunches, and a view you did not expect.

Good to Know

ETA. US tourists normally enter on an Electronic Travel Authority (Subclass 601): follow the official Australian ETA (Subclass 601) guidance before departure (about AUD $20), including the mobile app or web application linked there. Expect quick approval in many cases, and board with a chip-enabled passport valid for your entire stay. Digital confirmation is enough to travel.

Passport & backups. Carry the physical passport that matches your ETA, valid for the full trip. Keep phone photos (or a secure offline copy) of your passport photo page and ETA approval as backup.

Insurance. Medical care for visitors can get expensive; travel insurance is strongly recommended. Already have flights or hotels booked but still need coverage? I can help you place travel insurance as part of planning.

Money & etiquette. GST is included in displayed prices. Tipping is not expected but appreciated for standout hospitality (around 10% at restaurants is a kind gesture when service shines).

FAQ

Answers that help.

How long does planning take?

Read answer ↓

Most trips take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks to plan, depending on the complexity, destination, number of travelers, and how quickly decisions are made. If your trip is time-sensitive, share that in the inquiry form and I’ll be upfront about what is realistic.

What is the difference between the planning tiers?

Read answer ↓

Seven Feet Travel offers several levels of planning support depending on how much help your trip needs.

Tier 1 · Accommodations & Transport is for single hotel, rental car, or train reservations. There is no planning fee for this tier.

Tier 1B · Cruise is for standard cruise bookings for up to 4 guests, including cabin selection, dining and preference coordination, and shore excursion guidance. There is no planning fee for this tier.

Tier 2 · Multi-Destination Trips is for custom itineraries with multiple hotels, rental cars, trains, or several moving parts across destinations. This includes up to 3 hours of planning work and has a flat planning fee of $125.

Tier 3 · Expeditions & Complex Journeys is for full-service planning, including around-the-world itineraries, safaris, multi-country expeditions, or trips requiring deeper research, vendor coordination, and ongoing support. Pricing starts at $300 and varies by scope.

Group Travel · Hotel or Cruise is for room blocks, multiple cruise cabins, or coordination across multiple guests and rooms. The planning fee is 1% of the total value before taxes and fees.

Are planning fees refundable?

Read answer ↓

Planning fees cover the time, research, coordination, and care that go into building your trip and are generally non-refundable once planning has started. If something changes, I’ll do my best to adjust the plan where possible.

How many revisions are included?

Read answer ↓

Each planning fee includes a reasonable round of refinements so we can make sure the trip feels right. If the trip direction changes significantly, such as a new destination, new dates, or a different style of trip, an additional planning fee may apply.

Who pays hotels, tours, cruises, or suppliers?

Read answer ↓

Travelers pay hotels, cruise lines, tour operators, and other suppliers directly. Seven Feet Travel helps with recommendations, planning, booking support, and coordination where applicable, but supplier payments, deposits, cancellation terms, and final balances are handled according to each supplier’s policies.

What happens if my dates change?

Read answer ↓

Date changes happen. I’ll help adjust the plan where possible, but pricing, availability, perks, and cancellation terms may change depending on the hotel, cruise line, supplier, or timing.

Do you book flights?

Read answer ↓

In some cases, I can help with air travel, especially when it is part of a larger itinerary. For straightforward domestic flights on major carriers, travelers are usually best served booking directly. I’m happy to talk through what makes the most sense based on the trip.

Do you plan cruises?

Read answer ↓

Yes. Cruises are a core offering at Seven Feet Travel. I can help with cruise line selection, ship choice, itinerary, cabin type, dining preferences, shore excursion guidance, and pre- or post-cruise hotel stays.
